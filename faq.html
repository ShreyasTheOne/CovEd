Frequently Asked Questions

Who is eligible to register?
If you are a 6-12 student, you are eligible to register as a tutee for personalized mentorship and academic support from an undergraduate or postgraduate mentor.
What tutoring services are available?
We provide tutoring services for all school subjects from 6-12 (including some competitve exam classes), mentorship, and college preparation (including help with preparing for the JEE(M/A) or NEET exams).
How does tutoring work?
Due to the COVID-19 outbreak, our tutoring sessions will be conducted online. We suggest using WhatsApp, Facebook, although this can be decided on a one-to-one basis between the tutor and tutee.
I have signed up in the form. When should I expect to receive a match?
For students, you should expect a match in a week. Please send us an email if you are not matched within that time frame. If you are a tutor, we unfortunately cannot guarantee a time for which you would be matched.
How do I get matched with a tutor/ tutee? How does the matching process work?
After filling out the registration form, we have a team that decides the best match based on the preferences you have indicated in the form and any special comments that you may have provided to us via the form
How many hours a week do I have to commit?
We suggest students and tutors meet between 1-2 hours a week, although this can be decided among mentorship pairs. This may depend on the student’s needs as well as the tutor’s availability.
How long will CovEd tutors provide tutoring?
We expect tutors to be able to help until the end of the academic year for the student. After the academic year is over, discussions between the tutor and tutee can help determine possible plans and whether or not the tutoring will continue.
What are the responsibilities of a tutor?
Tutors are responsible for volunteering a minimum of one to two hours a week of their time to help their matched tutee with the subjects the tutee requests help in. While a tutor is only matched with one tutee, they can reach out if they would like to tutor more. Tutors are expected to help their tutee until the end of the 2020 academic school year.
How are you reaching students in disadvantaged situations?
We have an outreach team that is actively working on recruiting students throughout the country. In our registration form, we ask questions pertaining to socioeconomic status, allowing us to ensure that these students are matched first.
Can you help students that do not speak English very well?
Yes! We have tutors that are fluent in various languages and if this is a concern, be sure to mention this in the special requests/concerns portion of the registration form and we will definitely work to accommodate this. Additionally, we are working on translating all flyers and publicity materials into different languages to reach students regardless of their first language.
